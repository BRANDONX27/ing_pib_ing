MEXICO
In 2016 and for the second consecutive year, the product gross domestic product (GDP) per capita, or per capita, in Mexico kept to the downside, to be at a figure of 8.543 dollars per person, 10.0% below the amount reached in 2015.
From that perspective, Mexico is among the economies with the GDP per inhabitant, not only when compared with the developed economies, but also with some of lower economic performance.
It will take almost 50 years to double, the income per capita of Mexicans, in case the economy maintain its growth rate (2.5% per year).
In view of this, the Institute for Industrial Development and the Economic Growth (IDIC), affirmed that the next President of the Republic must undertake a change structure of the country's economy, with the objective of achieve growth rates of 5% and have conditions to raise the per capita income of Mexicans, which today in average, 8,200 dollars per year, that is, about 12 thousand 300 pesos monthly.
